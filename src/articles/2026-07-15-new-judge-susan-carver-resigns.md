Newly Appointed Trinidad Municipal Judge Resigns

The City of Trinidad has experienced another significant change in leadership following the resignation of newly appointed Municipal Judge Susan Carver.

According to statements made by Trinidad City Council Member Steven Strearman, Judge Carver was sworn into office by City Administrator Cynthia Dosier without the knowledge or approval of the mayor or city council. Questions surrounding that appointment have become part of the broader public discussion regarding how city business has been conducted in recent months.

Judge Carver also serves as City Secretary and Municipal Judge for the City of Brownsboro, making her appointment in Trinidad the subject of additional public scrutiny. She has now resigned from her position as Trinidad's municipal judge.

The resignation comes amid ongoing controversy surrounding Trinidad's city administration, including disputes over hiring decisions, financial oversight, governance, and the continuing Texas Rangers investigation into matters involving the city. Residents have increasingly called for greater transparency and adherence to proper governmental procedures.

Screenshots recently shared publicly by Council Member Steven Strearman also show what he says were text message exchanges related to the judge's appointment and resignation. Those messages have fueled additional public discussion about the circumstances surrounding the appointment process.

With another resignation adding to the list of recent departures, many residents are expected to seek answers at the upcoming July 21 City Council meeting, where continued questions about accountability and the future direction of the city are anticipated.
