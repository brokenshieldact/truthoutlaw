Trinidad Sees Wave of Reported Resignations as Texas Rangers Investigation Continues

Trinidad City Hall continues to experience significant turnover as the city faces ongoing scrutiny and an active investigation by the Texas Rangers.

According to information shared with TruthOutlaw, City Administrator Cynthia Dosier has indicated she is resigning. In addition, Sandra, the water clerk hired during Dosier's administration, has also reportedly stated she is resigning. Municipal Judge Susan Carver, who was appointed during Dosier's tenure, has already submitted her resignation.

These developments come after months of controversy surrounding city operations, financial concerns, personnel decisions, and questions about transparency. During this same period, the Texas Rangers confirmed they are conducting an investigation involving the City of Trinidad. The existence of that investigation has brought increased public attention to decisions made by city leadership and has fueled calls from residents for accountability.

For many citizens, the reported departures represent more than a change in personnel. They see them as an opportunity for the city to begin rebuilding public confidence. Residents have attended council meetings in record numbers, filed public information requests, and demanded answers regarding city finances, overdue audits, water system issues, and administrative decisions.

In recent weeks, council members have publicly questioned administrative actions, including expenditures made without prior council approval. Citizens have also continued to voice concerns over the city's management and have called for greater transparency in how local government conducts business.

While the Texas Rangers investigation remains ongoing and officials have not publicly released its scope or findings, the timing of these reported resignations has drawn considerable attention throughout the community.

Ultimately, the future of Trinidad will depend on more than replacing individuals. Residents have repeatedly said they want a government that operates openly, follows state law, respects the authority of the elected council, and restores trust in City Hall.

As these changes unfold, many citizens believe this could mark the beginning of a new chapter for Trinidad—one focused on accountability, transparency, and restoring confidence in local government.

Editor's Note: At the time of publication, the Texas Rangers investigation remains active. The reasons for any reported resignations have not been officially announced unless publicly confirmed by the individuals involved or the City of Trinidad.
